Cal Ice Hockey Gearing up for the Crown Jewel of the Season Schedule

After a road trip that included travel woes and four back-to-back games, the UC Berkeley hockey team will return home for an event that all Cal fans circle on their calendar, the Big Freeze.

This year’s Big Freeze will feature a white-out theme, a special guest puck drop, the Cal Band and Oski the Bear.

“The Big Freeze is more than a hockey game—it’s a celebration of resilience, rivalry, and community.” Cal Ice Hockey General Manager Michele Cox said. “For Cal Ice Hockey, it’s the pinnacle of our season; for UC Berkeley, it’s a unifying tradition that brings students, alumni, and fans together; and for the Bay Area, it’s proof that hockey belongs here as much as innovation and culture do. This game freezes time for one night, reminding us all why sport matters.”

UC Berkeley’s 12th Chancellor, Rich Lyons, will drop the ceremonial first puck for the Big Freeze on Saturday at 7 p.m., one week before the kickoff of the “Big Game” between Cal and Stanford football on November 22.

Cal typically hosts the Stanford Cardinal in the Big Freeze as a precursor to the Big Game. Stanford has opted out of the Big Freeze this season. Loyola Marymount University will take Stanford’s place and will provide a familiar look on the ice, wearing a crimson red and white jersey akin to Stanford’s cardinal red and white.

"The Big Freeze is Cal Ice Hockey's most exciting event of the year,” head coach Devin Cox said. “and one of the most unique spectacles that Cal supporters can attend. While having Stanford as the opponent is traditionally a major drawing factor — it is truly the way the fans show up and the players get energized that makes the Big Freeze as special as it is.”

The Golden Bears will enter the weekend matchup on a 10-game win streak and a 13-1 overall record. The team is coming off a four-game road trip last weekend that included a 16-hour travel day because of the government shutdown. Despite the delays, Cal defeated the University of Washington in two games and Western Washington University in two games. After the trip, Cal ranked 5th in ACHA's first western rankings out of almost 200 teams.

“From a packed crowd to a roaring Cal Band, with important symbols of UC Berkeley walking around like Oski and the Chancellor, it’s an excellent reminder that our hockey team is far more than just a club organization. We’re looking forward to showcasing just how exhilarating Cal Ice Hockey games can be,” Devin Cox said.

The LMU Lions have a 7-6 record and take on the San Jose State Spartans on Friday before two games against Cal at the Oakland Ice Center.

"The Big Freeze is the Cal Bears Ice hockey team's Superbowl.” Assistant coach Mark Arneson said. “The Big Freeze is Cal's most fun hockey event of the year. Bring on the Cal band, Oski, and fill the stands to capacity. Let us celebrate the tradition of the Big Freeze for many more years to come."

All fans are encouraged to wear white and arrive early. Both teams will take the ice at 7 p.m. The game will also be broadcast live on Black Dog Hockey.

- Ryan Carlson
